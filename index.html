WCAG Foundation

The 4 POUR Principles

Every WCAG guideline maps back to one of four core principles of accessible design.

👁️

Perceivable

Information and UI components must be presentable to users in ways they can perceive — including alt text, captions, and sufficient color contrast.

⌨️

Operable

All functionality must be accessible via keyboard. Users should be able to navigate, activate controls, and avoid time-based traps.

💬

Understandable

Content and controls must be easy to understand. This covers readable language, consistent navigation, and helpful error messages.

🔩

Robust

Content must be interpreted reliably by a wide variety of assistive technologies, including screen readers, braille displays, and voice control.
